==================================================================
DESCRIPTION
==================================================================
[size=6][b]OBodyNG Weight[/b][/size]

[i]Procedural NPC weight and body randomization for OBody NG — for women and men.[/i]

Every NPC gets a unique, proportional body — [b]no BodySlide preset library required[/b] — and equipped clothing follows the generated shape. Walk into a town and no two people share the same figure.

Instead of OBody picking from a library of preset files you have to collect and curate, this mod [b]generates the body directly[/b] through SKEE morphs, per NPC, for [b]both sexes[/b]. All logic, RNG and state live in a C++ SKSE plugin; Papyrus is a thin relay. Bodies are deterministic within a playthrough, so an NPC looks the same every time you meet them.


==================================================================
MAIN FEATURES
==================================================================
[list]
[*][b]Procedural bodies, women and men[/b] — women use CBBE 3BA sliders, men use HIMBO. Magnitudes calibrated to a real 1900+ preset library, so the spread looks natural.
[*][b]Seam-free by design[/b] — the NPC's real weight value is never touched. Body size variety comes entirely from morphs, so the head, body and worn outfit always agree: [b]no neck seams[/b] and no body-vs-outfit weight mismatch.
[*][b]Realistic + fantasy mix[/b] — most NPCs are grounded; a tunable minority are exaggerated bombshells / bodybuilders.
[*][b]Body traits[/b] — independent per-NPC features. Women: busty, perky/saggy, wide hips, hip dips, wasp waist, soft belly, thick thighs, thigh gap. Men: big/flat pecs, V-taper, barrel chest, gut, thick/skinny legs.
[*][b]Muscle tone[/b] — a tunable fraction of women are athletic, with visible abs, arm and leg definition, including a rare super-toned [b]"snu snu"[/b] Amazon. Men get tone automatically from their build. Body fat smooths it away, so it reads correctly.
[*][b]Male controls[/b] — a master toggle to turn the whole male-body feature on/off (let OBody/vanilla handle men), plus a [b]Male build[/b] slider to scale male corpulence to taste. Every part scales together, so proportions always hold.
[*][b]Unusual bodies[/b] — a rare out-of-distribution roll: ultra-petite/ultra-thick (women) or ultra-skinny/ultra-huge (men), disproportionate and atypical.
[*][b]Re-roll hotkey[/b] — aim at any NPC and press the hotkey (re-bindable, default [ / {) for a brand-new body on the spot. [b]VR-ready[/b]: in VR it targets the NPC you're looking at (HMD gaze), or yourself if nothing is in view.
[*][b]Clothing refit[/b] — armor built with morphs follows the new shape.
[*][b]Performance[/b] — distance-aware lazy loading drains the morph queue gradually and nearest-first, and bodies rebuild with the fewest passes, so entering a crowded cell stays smooth.
[/list]

[b]MCM:[/b] Body Shape mode (Procedural Morphs / OBody Presets), Distribution (Seeded / Random / NPC Default), Morph intensity, Fantasy NPCs %, Unusual bodies %, Unusual breasts %, Athletic women %, [b]Male bodies[/b] toggle, [b]Male build[/b], re-bindable Re-roll key, Bias and Seed. A FOMOD installer offers Realistic / Balanced / Fantasy starting presets.

[i]Light plugin (ESL-flagged) — doesn't use a load-order slot. Works on Skyrim SE, AE and VR.[/i]


==================================================================
INSTALLATION INSTRUCTIONS
==================================================================
[list=1]
[*]Install with a mod manager (MO2 / Vortex) and enable [b]OBodyNGWeight.esp[/b], loaded after OBody NG.
[*]The FOMOD installer lets you pick a starting preset: Realistic, Balanced, or Fantasy (all adjustable later in the MCM).
[*]In-game, open the "OBodyNG Weight" MCM and choose the [b]Procedural Morphs[/b] body mode.
[*]Recommended: in OBody's own MCM, set its actor-selection hotkey to [b]None[/b], so the re-roll key only triggers this mod.
[/list]
Your CBBE/3BA (female) and HIMBO (male) bodies must be built in BodySlide with [b]"Build Morphs" checked[/b]. Build your armors with morphs too if you want clothing to follow the body.
