[size=6][b]OBodyNG Weight[/b][/size]

[i]Every NPC gets their own body - automatically.[/i]

In most setups, the people of Skyrim tend to share the same handful of figures. OBodyNG Weight changes that. It quietly gives every single NPC their own body as they appear, so a city actually feels like a city: tall and short, slim and curvy, athletic and soft, lean and heavy. You don't collect, sort, or assign anything - install it and the world simply fills with believable, varied people.

Everyone keeps the same look each time you meet them, their clothes fit their body, and it all happens in the background. No menus to babysit, no setup per NPC.

[b]Who it's for[/b]
[list]
[*]You want real body variety without spending hours collecting and assigning BodySlide presets.
[*]You feel everyone in your game looks too similar - or like the same bombshell.
[*]You want a natural crowd: mostly ordinary people, with the occasional striking figure - not a town of clones, and not nothing but pin-ups.
[*]You want bodies AND physics that match, for women and men.
[/list]

[b]What it does, in plain terms[/b]
As each person loads in, the mod builds a body that suits them and remembers it. Most people come out looking natural and average; a smaller number are clearly slim, curvy, athletic or heavy; and a rare few are genuinely striking. Even two people of the same body type now look noticeably different from each other - no more copy-paste crowds. How varied, how curvy, and how common the "striking" bodies are is all up to you in the menu.

[b]Three ways to build bodies[/b] (pick one in the in-game menu)
[list]
[*][b]Procedural[/b] - recommended. Every body is built from scratch. The widest variety, and you need no presets at all.
[*][b]OBody Presets[/b] - OBody still assigns its presets, but each one is [b]re-fitted to that NPC's own size[/b]. Two NPCs sharing a preset now differ in size and fullness instead of being identical clones. Keeps your curated preset look while killing the clone army.
[*][b]Procedural Oriented[/b] - starts from a generated body, then [b]blends it part-way toward that NPC's assigned OBody preset[/b]. A slider sets how far. Your presets' flavour without losing variety.
[/list]

==================================================================
HOW TO USE
==================================================================
[list=1]
[*]Install it and load it after OBody NG. Pick a starting flavor in the installer (Realistic, Balanced, or Fantasy - you can change it later).
[*]In game, open the [b]OBodyNG Weight[/b] menu (MCM) and choose a body mode. [b]Procedural[/b] is the easy default.
[*]That's it. Walk around - NPCs get their bodies on their own.
[*]To set YOUR OWN body (or a specific NPC's), use OBody NG's own key ('O') - not OBW's re-roll key. OBW never overrides an OBody direct assignment.
[/list]

[b]Handy extras (all optional)[/b]
[list]
[*][b]Re-roll a body on the spot[/b] - aim at someone and press the re-roll key for a brand-new body for that person. Re-bindable; works in VR.
[*][b]Tune the look[/b] - simple sliders set how varied and how curvy bodies are, how common athletic or unusual figures are, plus the Natural / Curvy dials and an overall heavier/leaner dial.
[*][b]Women and men[/b] - turn each on or off independently, and make men slimmer or beefier to taste.
[*][b]Leave certain mods alone[/b] - tick a follower or custom-NPC mod in the Exclusions list and OBW won't touch its hand-made bodies. Or exclude one single NPC with a hotkey. Remembered across saves.
[*][b]Refresh everyone[/b] - one button re-applies bodies (and physics) to everyone around you after a setting change, with no reload.
[/list]

INSTALLATION
==================================================================
[list=1]
[*]Install with a mod manager (MO2 / Vortex) and enable [b]OBodyNGWeight.esp[/b], loaded after OBody NG.
[*]Pick a starting flavor in the installer - Realistic, Balanced, or Fantasy (changeable anytime in the menu).
[*]In game, open the "OBodyNG Weight" menu and choose the [b]Procedural[/b] body mode.
[*]Recommended: in OBody's own menu, set its NPC-selection hotkey to [b]None[/b], so the re-roll key only triggers this mod.
[/list]
Make sure your [b]CBBE 3BA[/b] (female) and [b]HIMBO[/b] (male) bodies are built in BodySlide with [b]"Build Morphs" checked[/b]. Build your armors with morphs too if you want clothing to follow the body.

==================================================================
COMPATIBILITY
==================================================================
[list]
[*][b]Body mods that set weight (e.g. Racial Body Morphs)[/b] - OBW controls body size/shape and will overwrite them. Height mods are fine (OBW doesn't change height).
[*][b]Preset collections[/b] - fully compatible. Procedural ignores them; the other two modes reuse and vary them.
[*][b]Your own body[/b] - assign it with OBody NG's own key ('O'); OBW leaves direct OBody assignments alone.
[*][b]CBBE 3BA required[/b] - plain CBBE lacks the sliders OBW uses. BHUNP is supported (set "Base body" in the menu if auto-detect guesses wrong).
[*][b]CBPC configs[/b] - OBW's per-body physics only applies with CBPC; delete OBW's CBPC files to use your own config instead.
[*][b]OBody "ORefit"[/b] - if bodies come out broken (all min or all max sliders, cloth clipping), try turning ORefit OFF in OBody's menu.
[/list]

==================================================================
FAQ
==================================================================
[b]Do I need to collect BodySlide presets?[/b]
No. In Procedural mode the mod builds every body itself. The other two modes reuse whatever OBody already distributes.

[b]My NPCs still get an OBody preset assigned - is it working?[/b]
Yes. In Procedural mode OBW builds the body from scratch and takes over from any preset - that's expected. In OBody Sim Weight and Procedural Oriented modes it uses OBody's presets on purpose, and varies them per NPC. If you want it to stop using presets entirely, pick Procedural.

[b]Do I still need armor / body BodySlide patches?[/b]
Yes. OBW reshapes the body you built; it does not replace body or armor meshes. Build your bodies and armors in BodySlide with "Build Morphs" checked.

[b]My bodies look broken - everyone is min or max sliders, cloth clips. Help?[/b]
Two common causes: you're on plain CBBE instead of CBBE 3BA (use 3BA), or OBody's "ORefit" option doesn't agree with your setup (try turning ORefit off in OBody, then Reprocess).

[b]Will my followers / custom NPCs change?[/b]
Only if you let them. Tick their mod in the Exclusions list, or exclude one NPC with the exclude-target key.

[b]My own character's body keeps changing. How do I set it?[/b]
Assign your body with OBody NG's own key ('O'). OBW leaves OBody's direct assignments alone - only OBW's re-roll key gives a random body to whoever you aim at.

[b]Does it change faces, height, or the NPC's weight number?[/b]
No. It only shapes the body, and keeps faces and outfits matching. Height isn't changed (yet); height mods can run alongside.

[b]Men too?[/b]
Yes. Women and men are both supported and can be toggled independently.
