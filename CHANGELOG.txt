Version 1.5.2

- Fixed bodies visibly re-morphing on their own from time to time. OBody announces every morph apply,
  and OBW's own rebuild could echo back into that announcement - each echo re-applied the body again.
  OBW now recognizes the echo of its own work (a short suppression window after each apply) and only
  reacts to genuine OBody distributions. Re-rolls, cell loads and outfit changes behave exactly as before.
- Added diagnostic logging (only when Debug Log is enabled in the MCM): every body apply and every
  source that queues one is now traceable, so future reports can be pinned down from the log alone.

Version 1.5.1

- Big men are finally BIG - without the spiky mesh. Measured 178 real HIMBO BodySlide presets and found
  OBW's male volume ceiling (a safety cap at ~1.12x) was crushing the muscular top end - real presets
  routinely push arms to 1.4-1.5x. Only the striking archetypes (Bodybuilder, Powerlifter, Stocky, and
  the rare "ultra-huge" outlier) gained muscle and scale - the ordinary man in the street is unchanged.
  And instead of one blanket cap, each male slider now has its own ceiling taken from what real preset
  authors actually use: arms and back go big freely, while the sliders that visibly break the HIMBO mesh
  into sharp/pointy vertices when over-pushed (body mass, belly) stop at preset norms.
- Much faster body application. The whole per-NPC morph set is now applied by the plugin in a single
  internal batch instead of ~110 individual script calls. Bodies appear near-instantly even in crowded
  cells, and the script engine is no longer bogged down while a crowd converts.
- Hotkeys fixed and hardened. The re-roll and exclude-target keys are now handled natively by the plugin:
  they work the moment a save loads (no more "key does nothing" saves) and respond instantly even while
  many bodies are being processed. Rebinding in the MCM works as before.
- Butt sizes are now a true gradation. The butt-slider stack scales per NPC from flat through moderate to
  gigantic (about 20% / 65% / 15% of women), and the overall butt-to-bust balance was brought back in line
  with real presets - fixing the "everyone got a huge bottom" lean of 1.5.0's fuller butts.
- The female volume cap was removed entirely (the proportions themselves keep shapes coherent now);
  the most exaggerated bodies are free to be exaggerated.
- BHUNP butts calibrated to BHUNP norms. Measured 475 real BHUNP presets: the UNP-family mesh reads
  fuller per slider unit than CBBE 3BA, so OBW's 3BA-calibrated butt fullness came out oversized on
  BHUNP setups. When your base body is BHUNP (auto-detected, or set in the menu), the butt-shape stack
  is now scaled to BHUNP's own preset norms. CBBE 3BA setups are completely unaffected.
- Your own character is never touched automatically. OBody also fires its "body generated" event for
  the player, and OBW could grab it and reshape your character on its own. Now the player's body only
  ever changes when YOU press the re-roll key (aiming at no one re-rolls yourself, as before). Assign
  your own body through OBody's menu and it stays yours.
- ...and old saves are cleaned up automatically. If a previous version already put an OBW body on your
  character, it is removed the next time the save loads (OBW morphs secretly STACKED with your own OBody
  preset - that's why your character could look inflated).
- Your character is now completely off-limits to OBW, period. The old "aim at nothing to re-roll
  yourself" behavior of the re-roll key is gone - one stray key press could silently reshape your
  character, and that body then stuck across saves. The re-roll key now only works aiming at an NPC;
  your own body is set exclusively through OBody's menu ('O'), where it always belonged.
- Fixed the "boot leg" and the pinched lower butt on big bodies. With the old global cap gone, a few
  sliders could be pushed past the range the mesh was sculpted for: calves inflating over the ankle
  (legs looking like boots) and the butt creasing at the bottom center. Each body slider now has its own
  ceiling measured from what real preset authors actually use (2,143 presets analyzed) - big bodies stay
  big through the sliders that can take it, and the ones that break the mesh stop right before they do.
- NEW: export any body as a BodySlide preset. Bind the "Export body preset key" in the menu, aim at
  someone (or at no one, for your own character) and press it: their current body is saved as a preset
  file that shows up right in BodySlide's preset list - keep it, refine it, build outfits with it, or
  share it. Works for women and men, in every body mode.
- OBody's per-NPC nipple and genital variation is preserved. When OBW took over an NPC's body it wiped
  ALL of OBody's morphs - including the nipple/genital sliders OBody varies per NPC, so every managed
  NPC quietly reverted to the identical base shape there. Those sliders are now copied over before the
  takeover and kept, in every body mode.
- Exclusion fixes. (1) Excluding a leveled-list NPC (a generic bandit/guard whose base is generated at
  runtime) silently did nothing while still saying "excluded" - it now keys the exclusion by the placed
  reference so it actually sticks, and honestly tells you when an NPC is fully dynamic and can only be
  excluded via its source mod. (2) Exclusion files now also accept the full FormID exactly as xEdit
  shows it (e.g. FE123ABC) - before, only the local part matched and a pasted xEdit ID silently failed.

Version 1.5.0

- Fuller, rounder butts - no more flat butts on curvy/thick bodies. OBW was only driving the base "Butt"
  slider and leaving BigButt, AppleCheeks and ButtClassic near zero, so butts read flat next to full thighs.
  They're now built the way real BodySlide presets do - stacked and scaled together - so a full butt has real
  volume and keeps its curve into the thigh, even on the thickest bodies. Measured against 2,100 real 3BA
  presets; the volume cap was also opened up (with the shape held safely below the mesh-break point).
- New "Natural women" body axis. A share of women get a more natural, less-exaggerated shape (a moderate,
  wider, lower, closer-set bust with a touch more drape; a less-cinched, wider waist; a softer, not board-flat
  belly) - a realistic <-> curvy dial derived from BHUNP presets. MCM slider (default 20%).
- New "Curvy women" body axis - the opposite pole (fuller, perkier, more cinched 3BA-style look), for BHUNP
  users who want some of that curvier shape. Off by default.
- Base body aware. A "Base body" setting (Auto / CBBE 3BA / BHUNP) shows the Natural dial to CBBE users and
  the Curvy dial to BHUNP users. Auto-detects from your load order; both axes work on either body.
- Clothed vs nude body. An optional "Clothed refit" (default 10%) gently trims the soft areas when an NPC is
  dressed so clothes sit better, and restores the full body when nude - OBW's own version of the effect, so it
  survives OBW's re-apply.
- BHUNP compatibility. OBW now drives both the CBBE-3BA and the BHUNP names for the few sliders that differ,
  so its bodies apply correctly on either body with no per-NPC setup.
- Faster body application. Removed a redundant second mesh rebuild per NPC, so bodies apply about twice as
  fast in the procedural mode, especially in crowded areas.

Version 1.4.8

- OBody can no longer "win" over OBW. OBody NG re-applies its preset not just on cell changes but also
  whenever an NPC changes clothes (and NPCs do that a lot) - so an OBW body could silently revert to the
  OBody one until the next area load. OBW now listens for OBody's own "body applied" announcement and
  immediately re-applies its body on top, every time, in all body modes. Your OBW bodies stick, period.
  (The player is left alone - whatever you picked in OBody's own menu for yourself is respected.)

Version 1.4.7

- Smoother body loading - no more stutter or "pop". Bodies now apply the way OBody does internally (the
  game updates the model on its own next pass) instead of forcing a heavy reload, so the brief freeze and
  the visible shape "snap" when an NPC's body loaded in are gone - especially noticeable in crowded areas.
- Exclude a single NPC, not just whole mods. You can now leave one specific NPC alone: add a
  "Plugin.esp|0xFormID" line to any OBodyNGWeight_Exclusions file, or bind the new "Exclude target key" in
  the MCM and press it while aiming at an NPC to toggle them on or off. Great for a custom-bodied follower
  you don't want OBW to reshape.


Version 1.4.6

- Fix: female NPCs no longer get a male body. NPCs that inherit their looks from a template (a lot of
  followers and mod-added or leveled NPCs do) were being read as male, so OBW gave them the male HIMBO body
  - and with the 1.4.5 re-assert it re-applied that wrong body every time they reloaded (cell change), which
  looked like the body "resetting" or being lost after walking between areas. OBW now resolves the real sex
  through the template, so women keep their female body and it sticks across cell changes.
- Tamer default physics (CBPC). The out-of-the-box jiggle was toned down - less exaggerated bounce on
  breasts, butt, belly and thighs, with a slightly firmer breast spring, for a more grounded default look.
  As always, you can retune it in the CBPC configs or replace it with your own.


Version 1.4.5

- Plays nicely with OBody now. If you run OBody as well, it used to overwrite OBW's bodies every time an
  NPC reloaded (walking between cells and so on) - OBW now reasserts itself on each load, so your procedural
  bodies actually stick. This only affects the Procedural and Procedural Oriented modes; OBody Sim Weight,
  and any preset you've put on an NPC by hand, are still left completely alone.
- The "exclude by plugin" list isn't capped at 128 anymore. The MCM Exclusions page is paginated now (Prev /
  Next page), so your whole load order shows up no matter how many NPC mods you run.
- New neck-seam colour fix. OBW gently matches each NPC's head tone to their body, so the neck blends in
  instead of showing a colour line. There's a "Neck color match" slider in the MCM if you want to ease it
  off or turn it off entirely.
- Tidier during normal play. The little debug pop-ups ("reprocessing...", "new seed", "regenerating body")
  no longer show up unless you've switched Debug logging on yourself.

Version 1.4.3

- Renamed the "OBody Presets" body mode to "OBody Sim Weight" - a clearer name for what it actually
  does: it keeps OBody's preset SHAPE and only re-applies it at each NPC's simulated body weight, so

- Manual OBody assignments are now respected. When you apply a preset yourself through OBody's menu,

Version 1.4.2

- Procedural now works with an EMPTY preset library. Previously the automatic procedural pass only ran
  after OBody distributed a preset (its "actor generated" event), so a setup with zero presets generated
  no bodies until you used the re-roll hotkey. OBW now distributes procedurally on its own: when an NPC
  loads in a Procedural mode, it gives OBody a brief moment to act and, if OBody never does (no presets,
  or an NPC OBody's own distribution skips), applies the procedural body itself. No effect in OBody
  Presets mode (that mode still relies on OBody's distribution), and excluded plugins / disabled sexes
  are still respected.

  Pear, Top-heavy, Hourglass, Voluptuous, Apple/Soft, BBW, Athletic, Athletic-curvy, Obese,
  Stocky, Petite, Amazon) that drives bust/waist/hips/belly/tone together. Replaces the old

  collider size follow the body's size and softness (bigger/softer = more jiggle + larger colliders;
  toned/lean = firmer/tighter). Uses CBPC's interpolation API per NPC; soft dependency — no CBPC, no
  change. Drop-in config files included (CBPCBounceinterpolationconfig_OBW / CBPCCollisioninterpolationconfig_OBW).
- Body-type API for other mods: OBW_Native.GetArchetypeId(actor) (0-14) and GetArchetypeName(actor)
  ("Pear", "BBW", "Hourglass"...) let other plugins read an NPC's generated body type.
- MCM "Reprocess all loaded NPCs" button: re-applies the current generation + physics to every NPC
  around you without waiting for cell reloads — handy after changing settings or tuning.
- Smoother vertices retained: the soft-cap that keeps big bodies from spiking the sculpted mesh
  still applies on top of the archetype deltas (no breaks/spikes).

Version 1.0.0 — Initial release

Procedural NPC weight and body randomization for OBody NG, for women (CBBE 3BA) and
men (HIMBO). Highlights:

- Per-NPC procedural bodies via SKEE morphs — no preset library needed. Deterministic
  within a playthrough (same NPC always looks the same).
- Realistic majority + tunable fantasy minority.
- Body traits (independent per-NPC features) for both sexes.
- Muscle tone: athletic women (visible definition) with a rare "snu snu" Amazon tier;
  men get tone from their build. Suppressed by body fat.
- Unusual bodies: rare out-of-distribution rolls (ultra-petite/thick, ultra-skinny/huge).
- Re-roll hotkey (re-bindable, default [ / {), clothing refit, distance-aware lazy loading.
- Full MCM: distribution mode, bias, seed, morph intensity, fantasy %, unusual %,
  unusual breasts %, athletic women %, body shape mode.
- FOMOD installer with Realistic / Balanced / Fantasy starting presets + an INI for
  new-game defaults.
- All logic in the C++ SKSE plugin; Papyrus is a thin relay.
